Origin of Chess

Chess, a timeless game of strategy and intellect, traces its origins back to the 6th century in northern India, where it was known as Chaturanga. This early version of the game mirrored the four divisions of the Indian military: infantry, cavalry, elephantry, and chariotry, represented by pieces that would evolve into the pawns, knights, bishops, and rooks we know today. The game spread to Persia, where it was called Shatranj, and from there it traveled to the Islamic world and eventually Europe. By the 15th century, chess had transformed into the modern game, reflecting the cultural and intellectual exchange across civilizations. Today, chess continues to captivate millions, from casual players to grandmasters, with its rich history and endless possibilities for strategic depth.
